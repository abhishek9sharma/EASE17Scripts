      <article class="markdown-body entry-content" itemprop="text"><h1><a id="user-content-presentzjs" class="anchor" href="#presentzjs" aria-hidden="true"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path></svg></a>Presentz.js</h1>
<p>Presentz.js is a javascript library for synchronizing videos and slides.</p>
<p>It's at the heart of <a href="http://presentz.org/">http://presentz.org/</a>, a website for freely publishing conference talks, pitches, lessons and the like. Presentz.org is free software as well, hosted on <a href="https://github.com/ffissore/presentz.org">github</a>.</p>
<p>There is also an <a href="http://ffissore.github.io/presentz.js">API reference</a>.</p>
<h2><a id="user-content-dependencies-quick-reference" class="anchor" href="#dependencies-quick-reference" aria-hidden="true"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path></svg></a>Dependencies quick reference</h2>
<p>(skip if you are still not using presentz.js but just reading this README)</p>
<p>Depending on which video and slide sources you are using, you need zero to a couple of additional js libraries</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="http://jquery.com/">JQuery</a></li>
<li><a href="https://code.google.com/p/swfobject/">SWFObject</a>: if your slides are made with flash or if they are hosted on slideshare</li>
<li><a href="http://developer.vimeo.com/player/js-api">Froogaloop</a>: if your video is on Vimeo</li>
<li><a href="http://mediaelementjs.com/">Mediaelementjs</a>: if you host your video files on your own. There are also .css files and images to setup a good looking player, so using medialementjs requires some additional effort.</li>
</ul>
<p>Since the following APIs cannot be bundled*, Presentz.js will load them on its own</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://developers.google.com/youtube/iframe_api_reference">Youtube IFrame API</a></li>
</ul>
<p>(*) It's either technically or legally impossible or unconvenient</p>
<h2><a id="user-content-a-json-file" class="anchor" href="#a-json-file" aria-hidden="true"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path></svg></a>A JSON file</h2>

<p>In essence:</p>
<ul>
<li>each presentation has a <code>title</code> and a list of <code>chapters</code></li>
<li>each chapter has
<ul>
<li>a <code>duration</code> (expressed in seconds)</li>
<li>a <code>video</code> with a <code>url</code></li>
<li>a list of <code>slides</code></li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>each slide has a <code>url</code> and a <code>time</code> (expressed in seconds)</li>
</ul>
<p>The most important information is the <code>time</code> of a slide: it's used to determine when a slidechange has to occur and which slide has to be displayed. Such time is relative to the video of the containing chapter.</p>
<h2><a id="user-content-events" class="anchor" href="#events" aria-hidden="true"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path></svg></a>Events</h2>
<p>You can listen to the events emitted by Presentz.js with the syntax <code>.on(eventName, function(){...})</code></p>
<p>Presentz.js emits the following events:</p>
<ul>
<li><code>slidechange</code>, emitted when the slide changes. Callback arguments: <code>oldChapterIndex</code>, <code>oldSlideIndex</code>, <code>newChapterIndex</code>, <code>newSlideIndex</code></li>
<li><code>videochange</code>, emitted when the chapter changes. See <code>slidechange</code> callback arguments</li>
<li><code>timechange</code>, emitted as the video player plays the video. Callback argument: <code>currentTime</code> (a float)</li>
</ul>

<p>At the moment, Presentz.js supports Youtube, Vimeo and raw video sources (as in the JSON above).</p>

</ul>
<h3><a id="user-content-rvlio-revealjs-dzslides-and-other-hashjavascriptbrower-based-slide-systems-that-can-fit-into-an-iframe" class="anchor" href="#rvlio-revealjs-dzslides-and-other-hashjavascriptbrower-based-slide-systems-that-can-fit-into-an-iframe" aria-hidden="true"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path></svg></a>Rvl.io, Reveal.js, DZSlides and other hash/javascript/brower based slide systems that can fit into an IFrame</h3>
<p><a href="http://lab.hakim.se/reveal-js/">Reveal.js</a>, <a href="http://www.rvl.io/">Rvl.io</a> and <a href="http://paulrouget.com/dzslides/">DZSlides</a> are some popular frameworks for creating very good looking HTML + CSS based slideshows.</p>
<p>As they monitor the URL to know which slide has to be displayed, presentz.js supports them by wrapping the slideshow into an IFrame and changing its url.</p>
<p>Each of these system has its own way of understanding which slide to display. See the following examples:</p>
<h4><a id="user-content-rvlio-and-revealjs" class="anchor" href="#rvlio-and-revealjs" aria-hidden="true"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path></svg></a>Rvl.io and Reveal.js</h4>
<div class="highlight highlight-source-json"><pre>{
  <span class="pl-s"><span class="pl-pds">"</span>url<span class="pl-pds">"</span></span>: <span class="pl-s"><span class="pl-pds">"</span>http://www.rvl.io/federico/presentz/#/0<span class="pl-pds">"</span></span>,
  <span class="pl-s"><span class="pl-pds">"</span>time<span class="pl-pds">"</span></span>: <span class="pl-c1">0</span>,
  <span class="pl-s"><span class="pl-pds">"</span>_plugin_id<span class="pl-pds">"</span></span>: <span class="pl-s"><span class="pl-pds">"</span>iframe<span class="pl-pds">"</span></span>
}</pre></div>
<p>where:</p>
<ul>
<li><code>http://www.rvl.io/federico/presentz/</code> is the base url</li>
<li><code>#/0</code> is the slide number (zero based)</li>
<li><code>"_plugin_id": "iframe"</code> is used to force presentz.js use the IFrameSlide plugin (otherwise the fallback ImageSlide would be used</li>
</ul>
<p>Reveal.js supports vertical slides, that are a sort of "sub slides" of a parent one. To show those slides, append the number to the parent slide number, for example</p>
<div class="highlight highlight-source-json"><pre>{
  <span class="pl-s"><span class="pl-pds">"</span>url<span class="pl-pds">"</span></span>: <span class="pl-s"><span class="pl-pds">"</span>http://www.rvl.io/federico/presentz/#/0/1<span class="pl-pds">"</span></span>,
  <span class="pl-s"><span class="pl-pds">"</span>time<span class="pl-pds">"</span></span>: <span class="pl-c1">10</span>,
  <span class="pl-s"><span class="pl-pds">"</span>_plugin_id<span class="pl-pds">"</span></span>: <span class="pl-s"><span class="pl-pds">"</span>iframe<span class="pl-pds">"</span></span>
}</pre></div>
<h4><a id="user-content-dzslides" class="anchor" href="#dzslides" aria-hidden="true"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path></svg></a>DZSlides</h4>
<div class="highlight highlight-source-json"><pre>{
  <span class="pl-s"><span class="pl-pds">"</span>url<span class="pl-pds">"</span></span>: <span class="pl-s"><span class="pl-pds">"</span>http://www.mozillaitalia.org/slides/linuxday12/#1.0<span class="pl-pds">"</span></span>,
  <span class="pl-s"><span class="pl-pds">"</span>time<span class="pl-pds">"</span></span>: <span class="pl-c1">0</span>,
  <span class="pl-s"><span class="pl-pds">"</span>_plugin_id<span class="pl-pds">"</span></span>: <span class="pl-s"><span class="pl-pds">"</span>iframe<span class="pl-pds">"</span></span>
}</pre></div>
<p>where:</p>
<ul>
<li><code>http://www.mozillaitalia.org/slides/linuxday12/</code> is the base url</li>
<li><code>#1.0</code> is the slide number (one based)</li>
<li><code>"_plugin_id": "iframe"</code> is used to force presentz.js use the IFrameSlide plugin (otherwise the fallback ImageSlide would be used</li>
</ul>
<h3><a id="user-content-plugin-override" class="anchor" href="#plugin-override" aria-hidden="true"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path></svg></a>Plugin override</h3>
<p>Presentz.js tries to understand which plugins fits best by looking at the video/slide url. If you wish to force usage of a particular plugin, add the slide/video json object a <code>_plugin_id</code> field. For example:</p>
<div class="highlight highlight-source-json"><pre>{
  <span class="pl-s"><span class="pl-pds">"</span>url<span class="pl-pds">"</span></span>: <span class="pl-s"><span class="pl-pds">"</span>http://www.mozillaitalia.org/slides/linuxday12/#1.0<span class="pl-pds">"</span></span>,
  <span class="pl-s"><span class="pl-pds">"</span>time<span class="pl-pds">"</span></span>: <span class="pl-c1">0</span>,
  <span class="pl-s"><span class="pl-pds">"</span>_plugin_id<span class="pl-pds">"</span></span>: <span class="pl-s"><span class="pl-pds">"</span>iframe<span class="pl-pds">"</span></span>
}</pre></div>
<p>Allowed values include:</p>
<ul>
<li>video: "vimeo", "youtube", "html5"</li>
<li>slide: "slideshare", "speakerdeck", "swf", "image", "iframe"</li>
</ul>
<h3><a id="user-content-adding-your-plugin" class="anchor" href="#adding-your-plugin" aria-hidden="true"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path></svg></a>Adding your plugin</h3>
<p>By calling methods <code>Presentz.registerVideoPlugin(id, plugin)</code> and <code>Presentz.registerSlidePlugin(id, plugin)</code> (before calling the <code>init</code> method) you can make presentz.js work with your own plugin.</p>
<p>If you think you plugin can be of benefit to presentz.js users, consider making a pull request and have included in the official distribution.</p>
<h3><a id="user-content-reference" class="anchor" href="#reference" aria-hidden="true"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewBox="0 0 16 16" width="16"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path></svg></a>Reference</h3>
